Supply - The amount of a good or service that producers are willing and able to sell at a certain price/law of supply - Other things remaining equal, as the price of a good, increases the quantity supplied also increases in a free market economy/Supply Schedule - a list of numbers that shows the relationship between the price of a good and the quantity supplied/Supply Curve - a graph of the quantity supplied of a good at different prices/Supply Curve's Move - up and to the right /Five factors that can increase or decrease the supply of a good. - Technology Resource Prices, Prices of Related Goods Number of Sellers Producer Expectations Government Taxes, Subsidies, and Regulations/Subsidies - A grant or contribution of money, especially one made by a government in support of an undertaking or the upkeep of a thing or supply or service/Demand - The relationship between a good or service price and the amount consumers are willing and able to buy./Law of Demand -

Other things remaining equal, as the price of a good, increases the quantity decreases in a free market economy/Demand Schedule - a list of numbers that shows the relationship between the price of a good and the quantity demanded/Demand Curve - A graph of the relationship between the price of a good and the quantity demanded./Demand Curve Moves - Down and to the right/Equilibrium - The point at which quantity demanded and quantity supplied are equal/Shortage - A situation in which quantity demanded is greater than quantity supplied/Surplus - A situation in which quantity supplied is greater than quantity demanded/
